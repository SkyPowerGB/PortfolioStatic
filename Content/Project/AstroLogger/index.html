Astro Logger

Problem

Astrophotography is done by taking several images of object to reduce noise also calibration frames are used to further reduce artifacts and noise. One such calibration is called "darks". They are fully black images taken with same settings in order to eliminate thermal noise . The uncooled cameras like DSRL this becomes a big issue cuz they need to be taken in same conditions usually same night. But timeframe at night is limited which can end up loosing valuable time for collecting light data. Cooled astrocameras can easly recreate conditions so they dont have that issue.

Another problem is with entry level model DSLRs and lenses they dont come with weather sealing making them vournabile to high humidity and dew . They also dont messure sensor temperature.

Idea

I got idea to monitor enviromental conditions . Arduino could messure temp and humidtiy plus output them onto SDcard data could later be associated with images trough EXIF date time . If camera was outside for while the internal temperature should be similar . While the internal temp might not is always same and is affected by things like taking images. Hopefully it should be close enough to perform calibration.

Ideally this would enable me to take calibration frames on "bad nights" while maximising time for taking lights (images of object)

This is super critical for multinight sessions , as object travels on sky the window could be as short as 1-3h .

Implementation

Device is made of:

  • Arduino nano

  • LCD 1602 module

  • SHT31 module

  • SD card module

  • RTC module

  • 3 pushbuttons

The code was made using AI to speed up development . After confirming code works the design was moved to pernament perfboard build. Finnally case was made using 3d printing.

Issues

After soldering boards arduino nano proved to not work for unknown reason. leading to its removal and placement of female header pins to make it replacable. Old arduino nano was placed in its place . In end it turned out to be issue due to old bootloader however both brand new arduinos work.

Due to uneven boards it prvoed difficult to design case so only first prototype was made.

Conclusion

While full review is still awaiting , it was great project that put use of existing parts
